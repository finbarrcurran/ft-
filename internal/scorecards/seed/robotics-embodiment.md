## 1. Scope

### In-scope
- **Humanoid / general-purpose robotics**: Tesla Optimus (as a segment), Figure, and pure-play public names as they emerge
- **Industrial automation & robotics**: ABB robotics, FANUC, Rockwell, Keyence, Cognex (vision)
- **Autonomous systems**: warehouse/logistics robotics, autonomous-vehicle stacks (where embodiment is the thesis)
- **Robotics enablers** where the company's thesis is physical-AI: actuators, sensors, robot-specific compute at the edge (overlaps AI-Infra `edge-silicon` — route by dominant economics)

### Out-of-scope (different adapter)
- **General industrial conglomerates** where robotics is a minor segment → Industrial-Electrical
- **The silicon/compute beneath robots** → AI-Infra-Semi (`edge-silicon`)
- **Pure-narrative robotics tokens/stocks with no product** → Speculative (the equity equivalent)
- **Defense robotics / drones** (≥50% gov rev) → Defense adapter

### Relationship to the AI bottleneck chain
This adapter is deliberately the **stock-side terminal of the same Scarcity Migration Map** the crypto/AI-Infra bottleneck donut tracks: GPU → Optical → HBM → ... → Edge → **Embodiment**. Where AI-Infra-Semi covers the compute *enabling* the chain, Robotics & Embodiment covers the companies *realizing* its final stage. A holding here is a bet that the AI buildout's end-state (physical deployment) materializes and that this company captures it.

### What this pays for
Robotics pays for **physical-AI deployment at scale** — automation that replaces or augments physical labor, with the upside gated hard by whether the technology *actually works in the real world at unit economics that make sense*. The thesis is a bet on real deployment + a defensible position (hardware moat, data flywheel, or installed base), NOT on the humanoid narrative alone.

## 3. The Eight-Question Adapter (/16)
Score 0/1/2, total /16. **Pass = 6+ pillars ≥ 1 with Q1 + Q3 emphasis; no pillar = 0.** A **reality gate** runs through Q1/Q4/Q5: pre-revenue narrative names will struggle to pass, by design — the same protection the Speculative crypto adapter applies.

### Q1 — Bottleneck / Deployment Reality *(sector-specialized — reality gate)*
Is there a real, binding deployment bottleneck the company is positioned for — and is the product *actually deployed*? Sub-criteria: real deployment scale (units in the field generating value — not pilots/announcements), bottleneck position (does the company own a scarce capability — actuation, vision, real-world data), addressable labor-replacement TAM, technology readiness (works reliably vs demo-stage). **Humanoid names with no real deployment score low here regardless of hype — this is the gate.**

### Q2 — Narrative *(universal)*
Embodiment/physical-AI/humanoid narrative — current/mature/stale. **Anti-narrative-inversion is at maximum here** — humanoid robotics is among the loudest narratives; penalize narrative untethered to deployment hard.

### Q3 — Moat *(sector-specialized)*
Sub-criteria: hardware/engineering moat (actuators, precision — genuinely hard), real-world data flywheel (deployed robots generating training data competitors can't get), installed base + switching costs (industrial automation's real moat), vertical integration. Industrial-automation incumbents have real moats; humanoid pure-plays mostly have aspiration.

### Q4 — Intensity *(universal)*
Capital intensity (hardware is brutal — manufacturing, not just code), R&D burn, path to unit-economics that work, cash runway for pre-revenue names. Hardware robotics has far worse intensity than software — score it honestly.

### Q5 — Visibility *(universal — reality gate)*
Real, recurring/contracted revenue visibility vs projected. Sub-criteria: actual revenue + growth (not bookings/LOIs), recurring service/software-attach on deployed units, order backlog from real customers. **Pre-revenue = low score here; this is the second reality gate.**

### Q6 — Sovereignty *(universal)*
Supply-chain (rare-earth actuators, China exposure), regulatory (safety standards for robots around humans, labor/regulatory pushback), jurisdiction.

### Q7 — Catalyst *(universal)*
Deployment milestones (first real commercial deployment at scale), unit-cost-curve inflection, major customer/automaker partnership *with revenue*, regulatory approval for human-adjacent operation.

### Q8 — Technicals & Risk *(universal)*
Reads 9e/macro. Robotics-specific risk: technology-doesn't-scale risk (the demo works, the deployment doesn't), capital-burn-before-revenue risk, narrative-deflation risk (when the humanoid hype cycle turns), single-customer dependency.

*None yet.* Likely first real candidates split sharply by sub-type: an `industrial-automation` incumbent (FANUC/ABB/Keyence) would score like a quality capital-goods name (real moat + real Q5), while a `humanoid` pure-play would likely fail the pass gate today (Q1/Q5 reality gates) and route toward Speculative-equivalent treatment until deployment is real. **That divergence is the adapter working correctly** — same as the AI-Agent crypto adapter keeping narrative out.

## 5. VETO / kill criteria
### Universal VETOs apply.
### Robotics-specific
- **Deployment failure** (the flagship product doesn't work at scale / recalled) → veto
- **Pre-revenue + cash runway <12mo with no path** → veto check
- **Narrative-only** (no real product behind the embodiment story) → veto → route to Speculative-equivalent
- **Safety incident** (robot causes harm, regulatory shutdown) → veto until resolved
- **Key-talent/IP loss** in a deep-tech hardware company → veto check
