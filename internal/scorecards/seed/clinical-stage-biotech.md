## 1. Scope

### In-scope
- **Clinical-stage biotech**: companies with drug candidates in Phase 1–3 trials, pre-meaningful-revenue (<$50M run-rate, per Pharma supplement threshold)
- **Platform biotech pre-commercial**: gene therapy, cell therapy, mRNA, RNAi platforms not yet generating material product revenue
- **Recently-approved micro-commercial**: just-launched with a single product ramping (transitional — graduates to Pharma at $50M run-rate)

### Out-of-scope (different adapter)
- **Commercial pharma** (≥$50M run-rate, patent-protected revenue) → Pharma adapter (LLY/ABBV live there)
- **Diversified large-cap biotech** with multiple approved products → Pharma
- **Pharma services / CRO / tools** (operating businesses, not pipeline bets) → would be a separate adapter (not built)
- **Pre-clinical / discovery-only** with nothing in human trials → effectively un-investable for this framework; flagged as Speculative-equivalent (too early to score meaningfully)

### Threshold rule (the Pharma boundary)
The clean line from the Pharma supplement: **$50M annual run-rate.** Below it → here (pipeline thesis). At/above → graduates to Pharma (commercial thesis). A name approaching the threshold can be flagged "graduating" and dual-tracked.

### What this pays for
Clinical-stage biotech pays for **optionality on a binary scientific outcome** — a drug candidate that, if it works and gets approved, is worth a multiple of today's value, and if it fails may be worth near-zero. The thesis is NOT "compounding" — it's **probability-weighted binary payoff with a runway constraint.** You're buying the expected value of a coin-flip (or several) before the cash runs out. This is the most option-like, highest-variance stock adapter, and the scorecard's job is to protect against the unforced error of over-sizing a binary bet.

| Sub-type | Code | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Single-asset clinical | `single-asset` | One lead candidate; pure binary; highest risk; hardest position cap |

Sub-type drives how harshly the binary-risk pillars apply — `single-asset` is maximally binary; `platform` and `late-stage` have more diversified or de-risked profiles.

## 3. The Eight-Pillar Adapter (/16) — Clinical-stage-specific
Score 0/1/2, total /16. **Pass = 6+ pillars ≥1 with B1 (science) + B4 (runway) emphasis; no pillar = 0.** A **runway gate** and **mandatory position caps** apply (§5) — this is the Speculative-discipline borrowed for equities.

### B1 — Scientific / Clinical Strength *(the core thesis — DOMINANT)*
Sub-criteria: strength of the clinical data so far (efficacy + safety signal in completed phases), mechanism validation (is the biology de-risked or novel-and-unproven), trial design quality (powered to succeed, clean endpoints), competitive differentiation vs standard-of-care. This is where the actual bet lives — score the science honestly, not the story.

### B2 — Narrative *(universal)*
The modality/indication narrative (GLP-1 adjacency, gene-therapy hype, etc.) — current/mature/stale. **Anti-narrative-inversion sharp** — biotech runs on hype cycles; penalize narrative untethered to data.

### B3 — Catalyst Calendar / Binary Map *(sector-specialized — replaces "moat")*
*Biotech has no moat in the usual sense pre-approval — it has a catalyst calendar.* Sub-criteria: dated near-term binary readouts (Phase 2/3 data, FDA decisions — PDUFA dates), the asymmetry of each binary (upside vs downside), partnership/validation events. Map the binaries; that's the structure.

### B4 — Runway & Financing *(intensity analogue — CRITICAL, gate)*
Sub-criteria: cash runway in months (vs time-to-next-catalyst — can they reach the readout?), burn rate, dilution risk (will they have to raise at a bad price before the catalyst), debt. **A great drug with 6 months of cash and an 18-month trial is a financing disaster, not an investment.** Runway < time-to-catalyst is a near-automatic fail.

### B5 — Pipeline Visibility / Optionality *(visibility analogue)*
Sub-criteria: depth of pipeline beyond the lead (does a single failure zero the company, or are there other shots), IP/patent runway, addressable-market size if approved. `platform` sub-type scores higher here (multiple shots); `single-asset` scores low (one binary).

### B6 — Sovereignty / Regulatory Path *(universal)*
FDA/EMA regulatory pathway clarity, orphan/breakthrough/fast-track designations (real de-risking), manufacturing readiness, jurisdiction.

### B7 — Catalyst *(universal — overlaps B3 but for the *imminent* one)*
The single nearest dated inflection and its setup — the next binary that will re-rate the stock, and whether the risk/reward into it is favorable.

### B8 — Technicals & Risk *(universal)*
Reads 9e/macro. Biotech-specific: this sector is acutely **risk-appetite + rate sensitive** (unprofitable long-duration assets — a risk-off / rising-rate regime crushes pre-revenue biotech regardless of science; ties to Pal). Plus binary-event-risk technicals into catalysts.

## 4. Worked example
*None yet.* By construction, scores here will be **lower and more variable** than commercial Pharma — a strong clinical-stage name might score 11-13/16 (great science + funded runway + good catalyst setup) but rarely the 13/16 LLY/ABBV achieve, because the binary risk caps conviction. A `single-asset` name with short runway should fail the gate. **That compression is correct** — the adapter is designed to stop a binary bet from masquerading as a high-conviction hold.

## 5. VETO / kill criteria + MANDATORY POSITION DISCIPLINE
### Mandatory position caps (borrowed from Speculative crypto adapter)
Given binary risk, theses scored here carry **mandatory position-size discipline**: a per-name cap (suggest ≤2-3% of portfolio) and an aggregate clinical-stage-biotech cap (suggest ≤5-8%). A clinical-stage name should never be sized like a conviction compounder, regardless of score. *(Exact caps to confirm at first use — flagged in open decisions.)*

### Runway gate
**Cash runway < time-to-next-meaningful-catalyst** → near-automatic fail / veto check. The company must be able to reach the readout that justifies the thesis.

### Universal VETOs apply, plus biotech-specific:
- **Trial failure** (lead candidate misses primary endpoint) → veto → re-evaluate from scratch
- **Clinical hold / safety signal** (FDA halts a trial) → veto until resolved
- **Going-concern / funding cliff** (runway gate failed, dilutive raise imminent) → veto
- **Fraud / data-integrity issue** (manipulated trial data) → veto → Exit
- **Regulatory rejection** (CRL / FDA rejection) → veto + full re-evaluation
